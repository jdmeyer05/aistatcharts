Thread: What is a Dueling DQN and why does it matter for trading? 🧠

Standard DQN: learns Q(state, action) directly
Problem: it has to relearn the value of EVERY action when the market shifts

Dueling DQN: separates into:
• V(state) = "how good is this market environment?"
• A(state, action) = "which action is best right now?"

Q = V + (A - mean(A))

Why this matters for trading:
The agent can learn "this is a dangerous market" (V is low) independently from "should I buy or sell" (A).

When VIX spikes, V drops for ALL actions. The agent doesn't need to re-experience every action to know it's risky.

This is why our model converges faster and generalizes better.
